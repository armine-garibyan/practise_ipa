Welcome

Practice IPA is an ongoing project aimed at first-year students majoring in English. It does not attempt to cover English varieties comprehensively, but only serves to provide IPA practice as the name suggests.

Features:

  • Progressive Difficulty Levels: Practise IPA offers two levels of difficulty. Choose between Levels 1 and 2, each presenting different degrees of mismatch between phonemes and graphemes.
  • Large Word Database: Practice with real and relevant vocabulary. Practise IPA generates random words based on your selected difficulty level, providing you with a diverse range of transcription challenges. At the moment, the database includes around 1500 most frequent words from the British National Corpus. Ultimately, around 5000 words will be available.
  • Instant Feedback: After submitting your transcription attempt, Practise IPA provides instant feedback. It assesses the accuracy of your transcription and offers the correct IPA representation, helping you learn from your mistakes.
  • British English Support (with more to come): Currently, Practise IPA focuses on British English RP phonemes, with target transcriptions based on the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary. In the future, American English will be added.

If you want to track your progress, you can create an account. Email information will not be collected (only username and password).

NB! For simplicity, /j/ and /w/ are listed under consonants, although they are often classified as semi-vowels. Also, since triphthongs are not given, you can build them using a diphthong and a monophthong.

Comments and suggestions are welcome! If something doesn't work as expected or you have other questions, please contact me at armine.garibyan@fau.de.
