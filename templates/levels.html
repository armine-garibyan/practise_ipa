 To begin practice, choose the level.

Difficulty levels are based on grapheme-to-phoneme mappings. In a word like bit, there is a 1-to-1 mapping between graphemes and phonemes (bit /bɪt/). This word would belong to Level 1. In contrast, you will come across a word like through at Level 2 since it contains 7 graphemes but only 3 phonemes (/θru:/).

Word grouping by level does not account for stresses (primary or secondary), length symbols (:) or clusters of phonemes (e.g., diphthongs or affricates).

Level 1
1-to-1 mapping
e.g., big, dog, problem, May, lamp
Level 2
less transparent mapping
e.g., youth, ice, psychology, write, station
